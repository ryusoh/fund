Optimized the rendering hot path in `js/transactions/chart/renderers/concentration.js` by replacing `.forEach()` closure allocations with index-based `for` loops.

Specifically, the `buildConcentrationSeries` function was bottlenecked by allocating a closure inside a high-frequency date loop and calling `Object.keys().find()` for every weight to resolve the ticker symbol (O(N^2)). This was replaced with aligned `activeWeights` and `activeTickers` arrays, eliminating both the closure allocation and the inner search loop.

A microbenchmark executing the rendering loop 10 times against mock composition data for 1,000 dates and 100 tickers showed a 98% reduction in execution time (5239.24ms -> 68.07ms).

Additionally, the `.forEach()` used to draw the line path in `drawConcentrationChart` was replaced with a standard `for` loop to prevent closure allocation inside the requestAnimationFrame loop.

Behavior unchanged.
